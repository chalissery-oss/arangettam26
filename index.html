The Art Form

About Bharatanatyam

"Where the hand goes, the eyes follow. Where the eyes go, the mind follows. Where the mind goes, the heart follows — and there lies the essence of Bharatanatyam."

Bharatanatyam is one of the oldest and most revered classical dance forms of India, tracing its roots to the sacred temples of Tamil Nadu. Born from the Natya Shastra — the ancient treatise on performing arts — it is a language woven from gesture, rhythm, and devotion.

Every movement speaks: the precise geometry of adavus (footwork), the eloquent storytelling of abhinaya (expression), and the spiritual surrender of bhakti unite in a performance that transcends entertainment — becoming an offering to the divine.

For a student, the Arangettam is the sacred threshold — the first public debut that marks years of discipline, devotion, and surrender to the art. It is not a performance. It is an initiation.

The Guru

Guru

Vidyalakshmi Vinod

Vidyalakshmi Vinod is a distinguished Bharatanatyam choreographer, educator, and performer. A senior disciple of Padmashri Chitra Visweswaran, she embodies both classical depth and creativity. She was recently honoured with the Kala Deepika Puraskar by Temple of Music, led by violin maestro Ganesh Rajagopalan, for her outstanding contributions to the arts.

In 2002, Vidya founded the Nrityalaya School of Dance in Washington, USA, with a vision to train the next generation not only as dancers, but also as teachers, choreographers, and cultural ambassadors. Today, Nrityalaya is a thriving centre for classical dance, with hundreds of graduates who have undergone over a decade of rigorous training in the Margam tradition.

Vidya has choreographed more than 300 works and has directed six acclaimed dance productions, widely lauded for their artistic and spiritual impact.

Shishya

Andrea Chalissery

Andrea Chalissery is a sophomore at North Creek High School and has been learning Bharatanatyam under the guidance of Smt. Vidyalakshmi Vinod since the age of six. She is deeply passionate about dance and has developed a strong foundation in the art form while continuing to explore her creativity.

Through Bharatanatyam, Andrea has grown both artistically and personally, gaining confidence and a deeper appreciation for the discipline. She is excited to share her passion through this milestone performance and looks forward to continuing her journey in dance.
